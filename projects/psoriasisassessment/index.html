Psoriasis Assessment

Using Computer Vision for Psoriasis Assessment.
Indian Statistical Institute, Kolkata (May 2018 - July 2018)

The goal of this Graphical User Interface is to automate the computer-assisted diagnosis of Psoriasis skin disease using Computer Vision. The functionalities included in the application is Severity Detection, Diseased Region Segmentation, Histopathology Image Segmentation and Munro's microabscess detection. Through severity detection we can predict the severity of the disease on three fronts : erythema (redness), induration (swelling) and scaling (development of scaly structures) on the diseased region on a scale of 1 to 5. The diseased region segmentation was aimed at segmenting the diseased region of the skin to detect whether the disease has spread to a greater area or it's severity has decreased. The Munro's microabscess detection detected the presence of neutrophils in the stratum corneum of the epidermis. An excess of neutrophils in the stratum corneum denotes that the person has Psoriasis.

We finetuned ResNet50 and MobileNet to perform the severity analysis through image classification. We used the very powerful U-Net architecture for segmentation since we had a limited number of training images. We used a capsule network to detect the presence of neutrophils for Munro's microabscess detection.
