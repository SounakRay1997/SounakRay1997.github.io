Online Writer Identification

Using machine learning to establish authorship of text written on a tablet.
Indian Institute of Technology, Guwahati (May 2019 - June 2020)

Supervisor : Suresh Sundaram

I worked with Dr. Suresh Sundaram for my Bachlor's thesis on this project. Through this project we propose a technique to identify the authorship of an online handwritten text using descriptors inspired by the Pattern of Local Gravitational Force (PLGF) from the area of computer vision. The strategy tries to leverage the spatial and temporal information in an online handwritten trace. The spatial descriptors are based on histograms that are generated by incorporating the pressure and velocity information encapsulated in the sub-strokes of a writer. We also utilized sequence modelling techniques to capture the temporal information present in the sequence of descriptors obtained from consecutive sub-strokes. By considering a sub-stroke as a basic temporal unit of a writer's trace, we used a Long Short Term Memory (LSTM) autoencoder to compress the sequence data. The compressed representation is then used to train a Support Vector Machine (SVM) to obtain the final authorship of the handwritten text. We tested the efficacy of our proposed approach on the BIT CASIA Database and achieved state-of-the art results at the text-line level.

This contribution led to a first author accepted paper submission (Oral Presentation) to the 17th International Conference on Frontiers of Handwriting Recognition (ICFHR) 2020.
